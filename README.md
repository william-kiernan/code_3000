I think AI ethics should be treated more like managing risks than just talking about what is right or wrong.
 AI systems can cause real problems, like bias or unfair decisions, if they are not carefully designed. 
 Because of this, it is important for developers and companies to take responsibility and think about these risks before releasing 
 their systems. I also think transparency and fairness are really important, especially when AI is making decisions that affect 
 people’s lives. Ai ethics all together is a very tricky discussion, as it seems today that its uses are unlimited so the potential for 
 harm also seems highly probable, so that is why I feel transparency is important, and to be very open because I think it is impossible
 to predict what can actually happen with their systems. Also when bad things do happen with AI I also think it is an intersting
 conversation of who or what is at fault, which is a whole other topic.
